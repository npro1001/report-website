Introduction

Cybersecurity is a key tenant to achieving organizational reputation, integrity, competence, and consumer trust. Information technology (IT) has become crucial to any form of institution and often is the beating heart for handling digital information. Universities are complex institutions with many unique facets that require intricate IT solutions. The prevalence of transitioning from cold paper storage to flexible IT solutions and management has been making rapid progress over the past decade. This major shift of how data is collected and used structurally must be secure by design and allow for only authorized individuals to access data. Too often universities deploy IT systems without building in security by default. This leads to a post-implementation of security, often resulting in a lack of complete coverage internally and externally. Incorrectly utilizing insecure networks opens up universities, such as the University of Central Florida (UCF), to liability from civilians and governments alike. IT security is an important investment into the secure future and reputation of all universities.
